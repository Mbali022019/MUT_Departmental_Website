About the Department

Shaping Tomorrow's Tech Innovators

The Department of ICT at MUT delivers high-quality, industry-relevant education rooted in community and driven by innovation. Our programmes blend rigorous theory with immersive practical training, producing graduates who are immediately workforce-ready.

Located in the heart of Umlazi, our graduates serve across South Africa's most prominent tech companies, banks, telecoms, and government institutions.

Academic Offerings

Programmes Offered

Three distinct pathways designed to meet students at their level.

ExtendedDICT-ECP

Diploma in ICT (ECP)

Extended Curriculum Programme
4 Years • Full-time

For students needing additional academic support. The first year bridges core skills before advancing into the standard diploma curriculum.

4 Years

Diploma in IT: Extended Curriculum Programme

NQF Level: 6 | SAQA Credits: Min 480 | SAQA ID: 96862

Admission: NSC with English (3) and Mathematics (2) or Mathematical Literacy (3). Minimum 23 points in best 6 subjects excluding Life Orientation.

Duration: 4 years full-time

MainstreamDICT

Diploma in ICT

Standard Programme
3 Years • Full-time

Covers programming, networking, databases, web development and cybersecurity. Includes a Work Integrated Learning placement in final year.

3 Years

Diploma in Information Technology

NQF Level: 6 | SAQA Credits: 360 | SAQA ID: 96862 | CESM Code: 06

Admission: NSC with English (3) and Mathematics (3) or Mathematical Literacy (5). Minimum 24 points in best 6 subjects excluding Life Orientation.

Duration: 3 years full-time

AdvancedADICT

Advanced Diploma in ICT

Applications Development
1 Year • Full-time

Specialisation for Diploma holders in software engineering, network security or applications development. Ideal for career growth or postgraduate entry.

1 Year

Advanced Diploma in ICT: Applications Development

NQF Level: 7 | SAQA Credits: 120 | SAQA ID: 117974

Entry: 360-credit Diploma or National Diploma in ICT at NQF Level 6, or equivalent evaluated by SAQA. RPL routes available.

Duration: Full-time 1 year | Part-time 2 years

Work Integrated Learning

Six-Month Final-Year In-Service Training

WIL Placement
Compulsory 6-month in-service placement in the final semester, assessed for credit towards the qualification.
Industry Mentorship
Paired with industry supervisors who guide project work, professional communication and workplace standards.
Networking
Routing, switching, protocols and secure enterprise communication for modern environments.
Practical Experience
Real projects, problem-solving, teamwork, and exposure to tools used in software, networking, and systems.
Professional Readiness
Workplace communication, time management, ethical conduct, and contributing to ICT teams from day one.
