About the Department

Shaping Tomorrow's Tech Innovators

The Department of Information & Communication Technology at Mangosuthu University of Technology is committed to delivering high-quality, industry-relevant education in ICT — rooted in community, driven by innovation.

Our programmes combine rigorous theoretical foundations with immersive practical training, preparing graduates who are immediately workforce-ready. Students gain expertise across the full spectrum of modern technology disciplines.

Located in the heart of Umlazi, MUT has a proud legacy of producing graduates who serve and uplift their communities. Our ICT graduates are found across South Africa's most prominent tech companies, banks, telecoms, and government institutions.

Programmes Offered

Three distinct pathways designed to meet students at their level and carry them to their full potential.

Popular DICT

Diploma in ICT

Standard Programme
3 Years • Full-time

A comprehensive programme covering programming, networking, databases, web development, cybersecurity, and mobile platforms. Includes a Work Integrated Learning placement to connect theory with industry.

3 Years Full-time

Diploma in Information Technology

NQF Level: 6
HEQSF Qualification Type: 63
SAQA Credits: 360
SAQA Qualification ID: 96862
CESM Code: 06

Rationale for Qualification
This qualification has been re-aligned to the new HEQSF and takes into account the latest ICT industry requirements. It reflects current workplace trends in ICT and ensures graduates have access to industry employment. The inclusion of a Work Integrated Learning component at NQF-6 is a unique feature that prepares students with workplace-applicable competencies.

Statement of Purpose
The purpose of the Diploma in Information Technology is to provide a career-focused professional qualification with industry-referenced knowledge, skills, and attitudes. Learners will be competent in IT systems development in distributed computing environments and choose electives in Software Development or Communications Networks.

Admission Requirements
National Senior Certificate (Diploma or higher) with:
• English First Additional Language (3) OR English Home Language (3)
• Mathematics (3) OR Mathematical Literacy (5)
• Minimum 24 points in the best six subjects excluding Life Orientation, including English and Mathematics/Mathematical Literacy.

Duration of Study
3-years full-time

Extended DICT

Diploma in ICT (Extended)

Extended Curriculum Programme
4 Years • Full-time

A foundational programme designed for students who need additional academic support. The first year strengthens core skills before advancing into the standard ICT curriculum.

4 Years Full-time

Diploma in Information Technology: Extended Curriculum Programme

NQF Level: 6
HEQSF Qualification Type: 63
SAQA Qualification ID: 96862
SAQA Credits: Minimum 480
CESM Code: 06

Rationale for Qualification
This programme expands access to IT qualifications for students who may not meet the cut-off criteria but demonstrate aptitude. The first year prepares learners for the diploma programme before they join the mainstream diploma in year 2.

Statement of Purpose
The objective is to expand access to IT qualifications and prepare learners for the mainstream diploma. After the first year, the purpose aligns with the Diploma in ICT programme, while providing additional academic support.

Admission Requirements
National Senior Certificate (Diploma or higher) with:
• English First Additional Language (3) OR English Home Language (3)
• Mathematics (2) OR Mathematical Literacy (3)
• Minimum 23 points in the best six subjects excluding Life Orientation, including English and Mathematics/Mathematical Literacy.

Duration of Study
4-years full-time

Advanced ADICT

Advanced Diploma in ICT

Applications Development
1 Year • Full-time

An advanced qualification for Diploma holders seeking specialization in software engineering, network security, or applications development. Ideal for career growth or postgraduate entry.

1 Year Full-time

Advanced Diploma in Information and Communication Technology in Applications Development

NQF Level: 7
SAQA ID: 117974
SAQA Credits: 120

Rationale for Qualification
This qualification is formulated to the HEQSF and reflects current ICT industry needs. It prepares graduates for employment and further postgraduate study while deepening knowledge in Applications Development.

Statement of Purpose
The programme develops ICT practitioners with a deep understanding of theories, methodologies, and practices in Applications Development. Graduates are prepared to manage software development environments and pursue postgraduate opportunities.

Minimum Admission Requirements
Candidates may enter with:
• A 360-credit Diploma or National Diploma in ICT or comparable Level 6 qualification
• A Bachelor's Degree in a related IT specialisation
• A completed IT/ICT qualification evaluated to NQF level 6 by SAQA
• Or via the institution's RPL process.

Minimum Duration of Study
Full-time: 1 year | Part-time: 2 years

Work Integrated Learning

Six-Month Final-Year In-Service Training

WIL Placement
Students complete a compulsory six-month in-service placement during the final semester of their final year. This placement is assessed for credit and helps students translate classroom learning into real workplace performance.
Industry Mentorship
Learners are paired with industry supervisors who guide project work, professional communication, and workplace standards while building a network of ICT contacts.
Practical Experience
The training emphasizes real projects, problem-solving, teamwork, and exposure to tools and processes used in software development, networking, cybersecurity, and systems support.
Professional Readiness
WIL builds career-ready skills like workplace communication, time management, ethical conduct, reporting, and the ability to contribute to ICT teams from day one.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the entry requirements for the Diploma in ICT?
A National Senior Certificate (NSC) with Mathematics and English at a minimum of 50%. Specific APS requirements are on the MUT website. Extended programme applicants may have slightly different criteria.
What is the difference between Extended and Standard programmes?
The Extended programme spans 4 years with additional academic support, extra tutorials, and bridging modules. The Standard programme is 3 years. Both lead to the same Diploma qualification.
Which programming languages are taught?
We cover Java, Python, C#, JavaScript, PHP, and SQL. Specific languages depend on the year of study and module. Students gain broad exposure across multiple paradigms.
Is Work Integrated Learning (WIL) included?
Yes. In the final year, students complete a six-month in-service placement during their last semester with a trusted industry partner. The training develops workplace skills, professional communication, project execution, industry standards, and mentorship. It is assessed as a compulsory, credit-bearing part of the qualification, ensuring students transition confidently into ICT careers.
What career paths are available after graduation?
Graduates pursue roles as software developers, network administrators, cybersecurity specialists, database administrators, web developers, IT project managers, and systems analysts across banking, telecoms, government, and tech startups.
How do I apply?
Applications are submitted through the CAO (Central Applications Office) at cao.ac.za. Applications typically open in April each year for the following academic year.
